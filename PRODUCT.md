# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: owners and decision-makers at small and mid-sized businesses and
organizations in Cameroon and Central Africa (Ambam and beyond) who need
custom software, websites, or IT services but lack an in-house tech team.
They arrive on a phone, often on a modest connection, sizing up whether
MMstack is credible enough to trust with a real project and a real budget.

The job to be done: decide, in one scroll, that MMstack is a serious team
that can deliver — then reach out (WhatsApp / contact form) to start a
project. Secondary audiences (international/remote clients, room-rental
prospects) are welcome but never steer the primary narrative.

## Product Purpose

MMstack is a Cameroon-born digital studio: custom software, websites, IT
training, and remote IT services, with a small secondary lodging/room
offering. This site is the vitrine — its entire job is to convert a
skeptical visitor into a qualified lead. IT services are the core story;
rooms (`Chambres`) are a minor, clearly-secondary section, never the lead.

Success = a local business owner finishes the page convinced MMstack is the
real thing and starts a conversation.

## Brand Personality

Bold and ambitious, but grounded. Three words: **ambitious, confident,
rooted.** The voice says "Africa's future is digital, and we're building it
from Ambam" — forward-looking and proud of where it comes from, without
hype or gimmick. Bilingual reality (French primary, English accents) is part
of the identity, not a translation afterthought. Tone is direct and warm,
never corporate-cold and never small-time.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI template.** The flat black/white + Inter landing page that
  looks like every other AI build — which is roughly the current state.
  This is the #1 thing to escape.
- **Cold corporate SaaS.** Soulless navy-gradient enterprise look with no
  human warmth or sense of place.
- **Cheap / dated local-business site.** Cluttered, clip-art, stock-banner
  aesthetics that undercut credibility.
- **Loud & gimmicky.** Over-animated, neon, effect-for-its-own-sake. Bold is
  earned through composition and color commitment, not noise.

The tension to hold: **bold without gimmick.** Striking, committed, ownable
— but every move pays its way.

## Design Principles

1. **Credibility in one scroll.** A skeptical owner on a phone must believe
   MMstack delivers before they stop reading. Proof over adjectives.
2. **Rooted, not derivative.** The Cameroon/Ambam origin is a strength to
   express through type, color, and copy — not a flag emoji bolted on, and
   not a cultural-cliché palette.
3. **Bold pays its way.** Commit to a real visual POV (color, typography),
   but every striking choice must serve credibility, never undercut it.
4. **Built for the real device.** The page is judged on a mid-range Android
   over a modest connection. Fast, legible, and beautiful there first.
5. **One clear next step.** Every section funnels toward starting a
   conversation; contact (form / WhatsApp) is never more than a tap away.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥3:1), full
keyboard navigation, visible focus states, honored `prefers-reduced-motion`
alternatives for all Framer Motion entrances and the looping scroll
indicator. Performance and legibility on small/older Android devices and
low-bandwidth connections are treated as accessibility, not just polish.
Content is French-first with English accents; keep both readable.
